## Design Context

### Users
Music fans and band followers — casual browsers who come to discover the band, stream music, browse photos, or find booking/contact info. They're likely on mobile at least as often as desktop. They appreciate personality and creative expression over corporate polish.

### Brand Personality
The name "Lovegods in Leisure Suits" signals everything: self-aware humor, retro flair, and a refusal to be boring. The Godzilla error page confirms this band doesn't take itself too seriously. The aesthetic should feel like it was made by people who genuinely love music and design — handcrafted, expressive, and a little over the top.

**3-word personality: groovy · irreverent · handcrafted**

Emotional goals: delight, nostalgia, genuine personality — fans should feel like they've stumbled into something worth sticking around for.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Era:** 70s–80s retro energy — think glam rock, B-movie posters, funky album art, avocado green and burnt orange, groovy type
- **Color:** Full, vivid palette. Not just black and white — warm golds, deep purples, hot pinks, mustards, teals. Pull inspiration from the existing album covers (Come Here Often, Dacron Hell, Groove On It)
- **Typography:** Display and retro headline typefaces with strong character — groovy, slab-serif, condensed, or era-specific fonts. Body text stays readable and clean
- **Mood board:** Indie/art-rock sensibility — quirky, visually opinionated, design-as-expression rather than design-as-function
- **Dark base** can stay but should feel warm and textured, not cold and corporate
- **Anti-references:** Generic band website templates, sterile SaaS aesthetics, anything that could belong to any band

### Design Principles
1. **Personality over polish** — Quirks and character are features, not bugs. A slightly odd choice that fits the brand beats a safe choice that doesn't
2. **Color with conviction** — Use the palette boldly and deliberately. No apologetic pastels or timid accents
3. **Era-aware, not pastiche** — Reference retro aesthetics without becoming a costume. The energy is real, not ironic
4. **Readable at all costs** — Fun fonts and vibrant colors must remain legible at every size and on every device
5. **Mobile fans first** — People browse at shows and on the go; mobile experience is non-negotiable
